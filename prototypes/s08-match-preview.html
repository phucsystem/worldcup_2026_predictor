Strengths & weaknesses

Switzerland
▲ Strengths
  • Defensive solidity. Akanji and Schär anchor a back line that has conceded just once in two matches.
  • Midfield control. Xhaka dictates tempo and keeps possession — they win the territory battle and slow games down.
  • Tournament composure. A settled, experienced core that rarely beats itself; comfortable managing a result.
  • Set-piece threat. Tall, organised on dead balls at both ends.
▼ Weaknesses
  • Blunt in the final third. Low conversion — they create chances but draw tight games they should win.
  • Passive when ahead. A tendency to drop deep and invite pressure rather than kill the game off.
  • Pace out wide. Vulnerable to quick, direct runners in transition.
Cameroon
▲ Strengths
  • Counter-attacking pace. Mbeumo and direct wide runners punish space in behind.
  • Physical front line. Aboubakar holds the ball up and is a genuine aerial handful.
  • Fast transitions. Onana's quick distribution launches attacks before defences reset.
  • Nothing to lose. Must-win urgency means full commitment going forward.
▼ Weaknesses
  • Leaky at the back. Three defeats in five; repeatedly punished from set pieces and crosses.
  • Midfield can be overrun. Struggle to retain the ball against a controlled opponent.
  • Discipline & concentration. Lapses and cards under pressure have cost them late goals.
  • Exposed by chasing. Committing numbers forward leaves space for the counter against them.

Storylines
